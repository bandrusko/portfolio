Miscellaneous Maps

This is a little gallery dedicated to projects that I had worked on both for fun and from my undergraduate degree. Take a look!

This map was made during my time at University of Wisconsin-River Falls. This map was initially made to be implemented into a story map regarding the negative aspects of urban expansion within my hometown. This map below shows all the plots that have finished development on it based on each year the landuse survey was conducted. As you can see, there has been a lot of urban development happening between those 16 years!

The map above was also made during my time at the University of Wisconsin-River Falls. For this project, I was challenged by picking any national park or monument within the United States and tasked to produce an aesthetically pleasing map of the park that was chosen. I chose Tonto National Monument since I had been there in person. I had used Adobe Illustrator to add some final touches to make this map professional quality!

This map was made during my freetime. While working on one of my labs, I had messed around with the chloropleth options and created this neat little rainbow map of Washington County, Minnesota. I usually show my friends this map as a talking piece, so I thought it would be nice to show in my portfolio as well!
